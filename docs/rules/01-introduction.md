# 01 — Introduction

> Source: Wares Blade Playkit, Chapter 1 (pp. 3–5)

## Setting in one paragraph

Wares Blade takes place on the vast continent of **Ahan** in a world resembling our own ancient or medieval history. What sets it apart is the **Sacred Writ**, a divine power known as **Wares**, which drives the iron giants called **Ryude**, fuels arcane rites, and empowers the titular Wares Blades — relics with many uses.

## What you need to play

- 2 or more participants
- Several 10-sided dice (D10), ideally 3 per player
- One pencil and character sheet per player
- A **Wares Maker (WM)** to lead the game (the GM equivalent). With 6+ players, consider an assistant WM.

## How a session flows

1. The WM begins the game.
2. The WM describes the situation the characters are in.
3. Players decide what their characters do.
4. The WM determines outcomes. If uncertain, an **Action Roll** ([Chapter 7](./07-action-rolls.md)) is used.
5. The WM relays the outcome.
6. Repeat 3–5.

When combat becomes inevitable, the same loop applies but with the more detailed combat rules ([Chapter 8](./08-combat.md)).

## Ending a session

The WM declares when the session ends. At that point:

1. The WM summarizes the outcome of the incident or arc.
2. Players are awarded a **Completion Bonus** to spend on improvements ([Chapter 10](./10-advancement.md)).
3. Players share feedback.
4. Character sheets are gathered up and put away.

Wares Blade is at its best as an ensemble story. Avoid letting one player or character hog the spotlight.

## Core game terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Player** | A person controlling one of the game's main characters. |
| **Wares Maker (WM)** | The person who moderates the game. Usually one. |
| **Character** | A fictional person in the game world. |
| **Player Character (PC)** | A character controlled by a player. Also called a **Maverick**. |
| **Non-Player Character (NPC)** | A character controlled by the WM. Includes monsters. |
| **D10** | A ten-sided die. `2D10` means roll two of them. |
| **D5** | A five-sided die — roll a D10 and halve the result, rounding up. (Equivalently, roll a D6 and re-roll a 6.) |
