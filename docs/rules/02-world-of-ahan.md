# 02 — The World of Ahan

> Source: Wares Blade Playkit, Chapter 2 (pp. 7–10)

## World glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Ryude** | Giant robotic armor handed down through generations. Used as weapons of war by Ahan's nations. The machinery is a closely guarded secret of the Artificers' Society (West) and the Sacred Seal Church (East). The artifacts that *control* a Ryude can only be produced by these two groups. |
| **Ryude-Master** | A character trained to operate a Ryude. Without training, the machine will not move at all. |
| **Word-Casting** | A unique discipline of Ahan. Resembles magic but relies on power generated by **Wares-Stones** sealed with the Sacred Writ. |
| **Wares-Stone** | A stone imbued with the Sacred Writ, refined for Word-Casting. |
| **Persona** | A special artifact containing one or more Wares-Stones. Word-Casters wear these to reduce mental strain; some types control a Ryude. Once paired with a Ryude, no other Persona can ever control it. |
| **Numenism** | A secret technique passed down to certain religious orders. Harnesses dormant energy in the human body. Two schools: easily-mastered, attack-focused **Numetic Force** and the more advanced **Numetic Arts** (devout only). |
| **Invocation** | A secret technique passed down to certain religious orders. Draws on spirits found throughout the world. Less powerful than Word-Casting, but still very effective. |

## Geography (Western Ahan)

Western Ahan is divided into four major regions:

- **Teela-tein** (west and north) — vast fertile plains fed by the Orgo River. Civilization dates back several millennia. Over a hundred nations make their home here, including **Dhakyte-Rasma**, the Great Northern Empire whose Ryude forces dominate the region.
- **Rumbqt Mountains** (central highlands) — soaring icy peaks populated by the remnants of ancient civilizations.
- **South of the Rumbqts** — flatter lands approaching the Sea of Gone. Settled about a century ago by exiles. Many new coastal nations.
- **Northern Frontier** — cold, heavily forested, almost entirely unexplored. Rumored home of a Drameah ("True Dragon").

A printed map appears on Playkit p.9.

## Inhabitants

The **Ahars** are the largest ethnic group in Western Ahan (~90% of humans). Yellowish-white skin, average height ~43 littes (172 cm). Other groups:

- **Femu** (~8%) — ruddy-skinned coast-dwellers; comparable height, stockier build.
- **Rasma** — pale, slender; from the Great Northern Empire.
- **Grim** — diminutive yellow-skinned, from southern Rumbqt.
- **Kaybress** — strange, hairless, deepest Rumbqt Mountains.
- **Fuvock** — yellow-skinned nomads, ~10-year-old child height.

Two non-human groups are also found:
- **Magu-Draph** — primitive swamp-dwelling lizardfolk.
- **Dyugiss Mhan** — dark-skinned subterranean tunnelers.

## Religions

The dominant faith is **Pegana** — polytheistic, eight gods led by **Maba-di**. ~300 years ago it splintered:

- **Holy Pegana** — seeks the divine through invocation of spirits.
- **Sacred Pegana** — follows Numenism.

Other notable faiths:
- **Church of Wares** — predominantly southern.
- **Neeyin** and **Ritch-Mahna** — pacifistic Numetic traditions.
- **Degmat Draggonas** — state faith of Dhakyte-Rasma; worships the Black Lord.
